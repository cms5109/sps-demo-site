Modernizing investor reporting

Here is the scenario: for the last two years, it's been a struggle to implement a CRM (there is no internal compliance); the website should be updated (partners don't believe that it is necessary); and the strategy for communicating the fund's performance lacks unification (completely lost on the team). However, all of this would be required of your portfolio companies. Sound familiar?

More intriguing information

Modernizing a company’s business development and communication practices matters for a host of reasons – the least of which is the potential danger in appearing dated compared to your peers. It is an important, albeit tedious endeavor – as we regularly hear from our clients. As firms that implement these practices continue to thrive, adoption will become more widespread – akin to the growth of dedicated PE business development professionals. The challenges associated with poor messaging and branding is not limited to potential deal flow, but also to investor and LP reporting. Currently, having a dedicated BD professional renders a significant competitive advantage, and a coherent messaging strategy is next on the list. It is no secret that competition is swelling, which is forcing LPs to request more details on past fund performance to predict future success.

The market is also putting a greater emphasis on deal sourcing and origination, further reason to focus on an integrated communication plan. Moreover, in a survey of deal sourcing mavens, more than half of respondents claimed that benchmarking tools and reports are important to their overall business development strategy. Earlier this year, Cambridge Associates released an interesting article, As Private Equity Evolves, Traditional Performance Benchmarks Will Not Be Enough, Says Cambridge Associates. Evaluations of PE fund investment abilities is critical to investors, and “investment-level performance benchmarks allow investors to gain new perspectives along several fronts (Cambridge Associates, 2017).” This includes understanding the funds ability to perform by sector, geography, and calendar year, among other metrics (Cambridge Associates, 2017).

Generating a set of criteria that highlights a fund’s deal sourcing effectiveness, compared to all PE firms, as well as your peer group, will portray a powerful message when reporting to LPs. At SPS, we feel this will become a standard industry practice in the years to come. The ability to showcase your fund’s success based on an objective, yet simple set of criteria will illustrate to LPs and investors that your fund has the capability to outperform the market.
